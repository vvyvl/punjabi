🌟 About Punjabi Samosa

📜 History

The samosa is a deep-fried pastry with a savory filling, originating from Central Asia and deeply rooted in Punjabi cuisine. It's a beloved snack across India, Pakistan, and by Indian diaspora worldwide.

🇮🇳 Cultural Significance

In Punjab, samosas are an integral part of celebrations, festivals, and daily gatherings. They represent hospitality and are often served with chai during family meetups and special occasions.

❤️ Why We Love Samosas

The perfect combination of crispy exterior, flavorful spiced filling, and the joy of eating them with loved ones. Samosas bring people together and create lasting memories!

👨‍🍳 Traditional Punjabi Samosa Recipe

🥘 Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups All-purpose flour
  • ½ cup Ghee or Vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon Salt
  • ¼ teaspoon Baking soda
  • Warm water (as needed)

For the Filling:

  • 500g Potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 100g Green peas
  • 2 Green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ teaspoon Cumin seeds
  • ½ teaspoon Coriander powder
  • ¼ teaspoon Garam masala
  • ¼ teaspoon Red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon Mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons Oil

👣 Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Dough:

  1. Mix flour, salt, and baking soda in a bowl
  2. Add ghee and mix until it resembles breadcrumbs
  3. Add warm water gradually and knead into a stiff dough
  4. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes

Making the Filling:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1 minute
  3. Add green chilies and cook for 30 seconds
  4. Add boiled mashed potatoes and peas
  5. Add all spices and mix well
  6. Add fresh coriander leaves
  7. Cook for 3-4 minutes and let cool completely

Assembling & Frying:

  1. Divide dough into small balls (about 1 inch diameter)
  2. Roll each ball into a thin circle
  3. Cut the circle in half
  4. Make a cone shape by folding the semicircle
  5. Fill with spiced potato mixture
  6. Seal the top by pressing edges
  7. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown
  8. Drain on paper towels

💡 Pro Tips:

  • Keep the dough stiff to make folding easier
  • Oil should be hot enough (around 350°F) for perfect crispiness
  • Don't overfill the samosa as it may burst during frying
  • Let the filling cool before filling samosas
  • Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce

🫶 Chutney Guide

🌿 Mint Chutney

Ingredients: Fresh mint, coriander, green chilies, coconut, lemon juice, salt

Best with: Samosas, pakoras, spring rolls

Flavor: Cool, refreshing, and tangy with a spicy kick

🍋 Tamarind Chutney

Ingredients: Tamarind, jaggery, cumin, ginger, salt, red chili powder

Best with: All snacks and street food

Flavor: Sweet, sour, and mildly spicy

🌶️ Red Chili Chutney

Ingredients: Red chilies, garlic, cumin seeds, salt, oil

Best with: For spice lovers

Flavor: Bold, fiery, and intensely spicy
